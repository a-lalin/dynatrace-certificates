## [Metrics](https://docs.dynatrace.com/docs/analyze-explore-automate/metrics)

> **[Metrics Classic](https://docs.dynatrace.com/docs/analyze-explore-automate/metrics-classic)** **[Built-in Metrics on Grail](https://docs.dynatrace.com/docs/analyze-explore-automate/metrics/built-in-metrics-on-grail)**

Numerical measurements collected over time — like **CPU**, **memory**, **response times**, **error rates**, or **active user counts**.

Help paint a big-picture view of system health and performance. Spikes or drops can signal that something is off.

## Logs

Capture **events**: who did what, when it happened, and what the system reported about it.

> e.g. a successful login, a failed payment, an internal error message.

## [Log Content Analysis](https://docs.dynatrace.com/docs/analyze-explore-automate/logs/lma-analysis)

Log Management and Analytics gives you direct access to the log content of all your system's mission-critical processes. Log data typically contains a lot of information — one way to handle large volumes is to **narrow down log records** and **parse** them.

Logs can be filtered by:

- **Log level**
- **Keywords or phrases**
- **Security content**

> [!NOTE] Security content can be used to **restrict access** based on user roles.

## Traces

Follow the path of a **single request** as it moves through an application — from the moment a user clicks "Buy Now" to the very last system it touches.

Tracing shows **where time is spent** along the way:

- action on the web server (1 sec)
- action on the middleware server (1 sec)
- action on the database (4 sec) — that's not normal, and now we know where to look

Traces are made of **spans**, which represent the **units of work** within a distributed trace.

## Logs and Traces App

Dynatrace has dedicated apps for **Logs** and **Traces**. These apps let you explore massive amounts of data pulled from Grail, and can also **generate DQL queries** for you — helpful when you're still getting comfortable with the language.

Filters available in both apps include **log level**, **keywords or phrases**, and **security content**.
